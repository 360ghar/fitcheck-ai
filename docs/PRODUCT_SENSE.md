# Product sense

Last updated: 2026-07-22

## Who it is for

People who want less friction deciding what to wear: building a digital wardrobe from photos, generating outfits, planning against calendar/weather, and optionally trying virtual try-on, photoshoot-style images, and social sharing.

## Jobs to be done

1. **Capture the wardrobe** — upload photos; AI extracts items with usable attributes and images.  
2. **Decide an outfit** — compose, generate, or recommend looks that fit context (occasion, weather, calendar).  
3. **Trust the result** — clear review before save; originals usable when studio images lag.  
4. **Return habitually** — planning, streaks/gamification, referrals/subscriptions without blocking core value.

## Product principles

- **Photo-first, low typing** — extraction and generation should reduce manual cataloging.  
- **Review before commit** — AI proposals are drafts; users confirm wardrobe/outfit writes.  
- **Cross-platform parity where it matters** — web and mobile share backend truth; UX may differ by device.  
- **Honest AI limits** — prefer recoverable failures and clear status (batch SSE, job pills) over silent wrongness.  
- **Privacy-aware defaults** — user-owned wardrobe data; share flows are explicit.

## Non-goals (current)

- Being a full fashion marketplace (shopping is assistive/gap-oriented, not the core).  
- Replacing professional stylists for every user segment.  
- Local-offline-first architecture.
